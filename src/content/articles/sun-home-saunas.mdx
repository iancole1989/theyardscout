## The brand — what Sun Home actually is

Sun Home Saunas (sometimes branded "Sun Home Health") is a US-based infrared sauna brand that grew rapidly in the late 2010s and early 2020s. Direct-to-consumer model, no retail dealer network (similar to Casper / Tuft & Needle mattress model adapted to sauna).

**Manufacturing reality:**
- Designed in US (California-based company)
- Manufactured offshore (typical for the mid-tier sauna market — Chinese cabin construction is standard at this price)
- Direct shipping to consumer (no dealer network markup)
- Warranty and customer service US-based

**Market position:**
- Above budget brands (JNH, Dynamic, Maxxus) on materials quality, EMF spec, and warranty
- Below premium brands (Clearlight, Sunlighten) on EMF spec, warranty terms, and brand recognition
- Roughly comparable to mid-tier offerings from Almost Heaven and Higher Dose Infrared

Most buyers in the $3,500-6,000 budget range consider Sun Home as one of 2-3 finalist brands alongside Clearlight Aspire and possibly Higher Dose Infrared.

## Sun Home lineup — what's actually offered

### Solstice collection (entry-premium, $3,000-4,500)

Sun Home's most popular line. 1-2 person cabin saunas with full-spectrum infrared.

- **Solstice 1**: 1-person, ~$3,000-3,500. Smallest footprint.
- **Solstice 2**: 1-2 person, ~$3,500-4,200. Slightly wider; one person can recline.
- **Solstice 3**: 2-person seated, ~$4,000-4,500. Sweet-spot of the lineup.

What you get across Solstice:
- Full-spectrum infrared panels (no upgrade required)
- Canadian hemlock construction (premium hemlock from Pacific Northwest)
- 5-year cabin warranty, 7-year heater warranty
- Bluetooth audio, chromotherapy lighting standard
- Plug-and-play 120V household outlet

### Equinox collection (mid-tier, $4,500-6,000)

Equinox is Sun Home's larger 2-3 person cabin. More space per user, more panels for comprehensive infrared coverage.

- **Equinox 2**: 2-person, ~$4,500-5,200
- **Equinox 3**: 3-person, ~$5,200-6,000

Adds vs Solstice:
- Larger interior (more space per user)
- More infrared panels (more comprehensive coverage)
- Premium control panel options
- Same warranty as Solstice

### Luminar collection (premium, $5,500-7,500)

Sun Home's premium tier — closer to Clearlight Aspire pricing with premium aesthetics.

- **Luminar 1**: 1-2 person, ~$5,500-6,500
- **Luminar 2**: 2-3 person, ~$6,500-7,500

Upgrades vs Equinox:
- Higher-grade Canadian cedar (vs hemlock standard)
- More premium control panel + integration
- Lifetime electrical warranty (otherwise same)
- More refined exterior finishing

### Outdoor models

Sun Home offers limited outdoor models at $6,000-9,000 — designed for outdoor installation. Less common purchase; most Sun Home buyers go indoor.

## EMF specification reality

EMF (electromagnetic field) levels at bench are the single most-cited Sun Home specification by both fans and critics. Honest assessment:

| Brand tier | Typical EMF at bench (mG) |
|---|---|
| Budget (JNH Lifestyles, generic Amazon) | 5-30+ mG |
| **Sun Home** | **2-8 mG** |
| Higher Dose Infrared | 1-4 mG |
| Sunlighten | 0-3 mG |
| Clearlight | 0-1 mG |

Sun Home publishes third-party EMF test results, which is good practice and uncommon in the budget tier. The 2-8 mG range is acceptable for most users; EMF-sensitive individuals should pay the premium for Clearlight (0-1 mG range).

For context: ambient EMF in a typical home is 1-5 mG (electronics, wiring). A 2-8 mG sauna is in the same range as standing near a microwave or laptop. Whether this matters health-wise is debated; conservative consumers pay the premium for lower spec.

## Build quality

After tracking Sun Home owner reports for ~3 years:

**Strengths:**
- Full-spectrum infrared panels standard across all current lines
- Canadian hemlock (or cedar on Luminar) — real wood, not veneer
- 5-year cabin / 7-year heater warranty is generous for the price point
- Direct-to-consumer pricing beats dealer-network brands at equivalent quality
- US-based customer service responsive in reported cases

**Watch-outs:**
- Manufacturing variation: small percentage of units arrive with cosmetic issues (chipped trim, small alignment issues). Sun Home generally handles via return/replacement.
- Cabin tongue-and-groove fit is acceptable but not premium — small visible seams between boards on some units
- Heater controls are mid-tier (vs Clearlight's premium controls + app integration)
- Assembly takes 3-5 hours with 2 people

## Sun Home vs alternatives

The most common comparison.

| Factor | Sun Home Solstice 3 | Clearlight Aspire 2 |

| Capacity | 2-person seated | 1-2 person |
| Full-spectrum | Yes | Yes |
| EMF spec | 2-8 mG | 0-1 mG |
| Wood | Canadian hemlock | Western Red Cedar or basswood |
| Cabin warranty | 5 years | Lifetime |
| Heater warranty | 7 years | Lifetime |

**Choose Clearlight if:** EMF spec matters, lifetime warranty matters, US-manufacturing premium matters, $500-1,000 premium acceptable.

**Choose Sun Home if:** $500-1,000 savings matters more than the marginal EMF improvement and warranty length difference. Both deliver real-cedar full-spectrum sauna; Clearlight has tighter specs across the board.

### Sun Home vs Sunlighten

Sunlighten is the other premium infrared brand. Side-by-side:

| Factor | Sun Home Equinox 2 | Sunlighten Signature II |

Sunlighten edges out on EMF and brand recognition. Sun Home edges out on price. Most buyers prefer one based on local-area marketing exposure rather than meaningful spec differences.

### Sun Home vs JNH Lifestyles (budget alternative)

JNH is the most common "cheaper Sun Home alternative" buyers consider.

| Factor | Sun Home Solstice 2 | JNH equivalent |
|---|---|---|
| EMF spec | 2-8 mG | 8-15 mG |
| Wood quality | Canadian hemlock | Hemlock (lower grade) |
| Warranty | 5 yr / 7 yr | 5 yr / 5 yr |

JNH wins on price by $1,000-1,500. Sun Home wins on EMF, build quality, and heater warranty. For regular users (3+ sessions/week), Sun Home is the operator pick. For occasional users, JNH delivers the experience at lower price.

## Install reality

Sun Home models are designed for indoor installation, plug-and-play 120V household outlets. No electrician required for most setups — significant cost savings vs traditional steam saunas (which need 240V).

Requirements:
- **Dedicated 20A circuit recommended** (15A works for Solstice 1-2; Equinox/Luminar prefer 20A for reliability)
- **Floor space**: footprint + 6 inches clearance on all sides
- **Ceiling clearance**: 7+ ft for standard models
- **Floor surface**: stable, level. Carpet works; tile/wood is better.

Assembly is 3-5 hours with 2 people. The cabin pieces snap together; no drilling into cabin walls. Most owners report straightforward assembly.

## Bottom line

Sun Home Saunas is the operator-honest mid-tier pick in the infrared sauna market. Real Canadian hemlock or cedar, full-spectrum infrared standard, 5-year / 7-year warranty, direct-to-consumer pricing. The Solstice 2-3 at $3,500-4,500 is the most-popular individual model and a defensible choice for most regular infrared users.

For tight budgets ($2,500 or less): JNH Lifestyles is the budget alternative. For EMF-sensitive users or lifetime warranty seekers: pay the Clearlight premium. For premium-aesthetic priority: Clearlight Sanctuary or Sunlighten Amplify lines.
