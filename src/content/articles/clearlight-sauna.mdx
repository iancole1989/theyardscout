## The brand — what Clearlight actually is

Clearlight Saunas is the consumer brand of Healthy Wave LLC, based in Idaho. They've been manufacturing infrared saunas since the early 2000s, making them one of the longest-tenured US infrared brands. The brand specifically positions on:

1. **Low EMF engineering** — heating panels and electrical components designed to minimize electromagnetic field exposure at bench level
2. **Full-spectrum infrared** — far + mid + near infrared wavelengths in standard models (no upgrade required)
3. **Real wood construction** — Western Red Cedar or premium basswood depending on model line
4. **Lifetime warranty** — strongest in the category for the original purchaser

**Position in the infrared market:**

- **Above mainstream brands** (JNH, Dynamic, Maxxus) on EMF engineering, build quality, warranty, and price
- **Comparable to Sunlighten** on premium tier — direct competitor, different aesthetic and slightly different feature emphasis
- **Above Sun Home Saunas** on premium-tier features and warranty; Sun Home is the mid-tier alternative

Most US-market premium infrared buyers consider Clearlight as one of 2-3 finalist brands (alongside Sunlighten and sometimes Higher Dose).

## Real differentiators — beyond the marketing

### Low EMF engineering

This is where Clearlight earns the premium tier price genuinely.

Standard EMF levels at bench (measurement at 6 inches from heating panel during operation):

| Brand tier | Typical EMF (milligauss) |
| --- | --- |
| Generic Amazon infrared | 20-100+ mG |
| Mid-tier (JNH, Dynamic) | 5-15 mG |
| Sun Home | 2-8 mG |
| Sunlighten | 0-3 mG |
| **Clearlight** | **0-1 mG** |

For a 30-45 minute session 3+ times per week, the cumulative EMF exposure compounds. Clearlight's engineering specifically targets near-zero EMF — they publish independent third-party test results, which most competitors don't.

Whether EMF exposure at these levels matters health-wise is debated. The conservative interpretation: if you're going to sit in a heating element for thousands of hours over a decade of ownership, minimize exposure where you can without sacrificing function. Clearlight is the easiest way to do that.

### Full-spectrum panels standard

Many brands charge a premium for full-spectrum (vs far-infrared-only) panels. Clearlight makes full-spectrum the standard across current model lines — no upgrade SKU, no add-on cost.

Full-spectrum delivers far + mid + near infrared wavelengths in a single panel. Practical effect: more comprehensive infrared exposure across the session, potentially deeper penetration (research is mixed but the operator preference is real).

### Lifetime warranty

The genuinely strongest warranty in the infrared sauna category:

- **Lifetime on heaters** for the original purchaser
- **Lifetime on the cabin/wood** structural elements
- **Lifetime on electrical** components
- **5 years on speakers and chromotherapy** (slightly shorter — entertainment-electronics tier)

The warranty is non-transferable, so it covers the original purchaser only. But for the original purchaser, it materially de-risks the long-term ownership. A heating panel failure at year 12 costs nothing.

Most competitor brands warranty heaters for 5-10 years and cabin for 5-7 years.

## Install reality

Clearlight indoor saunas plug into standard 120V household outlets — no electrician required for most indoor models. This is a meaningful install advantage over some competitor brands that need 240V hardwiring.

**Requirements:**
- **Dedicated 20A circuit recommended** (15A works but may trip if other appliances share the circuit)
- **Floor space**: footprint per model spec + ~6 inches clearance on all sides for ventilation
- **Ceiling clearance**: most models need 7+ ft ceiling height; Sanctuary Y needs 8 ft for the yoga-tall ceiling
- **Floor surface**: stable, level. Carpet is fine for indoor models. Tile/wood is best.
- **No special ventilation required** for indoor models (cabin is sealed; no external venting)

**Outdoor models** (Sanctuary Retreat) require:
- Concrete pad or load-rated deck
- Weather-protected location (or sufficient overhang)
- 240V wiring for the outdoor electrical (heavier load + weather protection)

Most buyers get the Clearlight set up in a basement, dedicated wellness room, garage, or large bathroom. Assembly is 3-6 hours with 2 people (the cabin pieces snap together; no drilling or screws into the cabin walls).

## Where to buy

- **Clearlight direct** (healthywavemats.com / clearlight.com) — primary channel. Best warranty experience, regular promo discounts.

## Bottom line

Clearlight is the operator-honest premium pick in the infrared sauna category. The combination of best-in-class EMF engineering, lifetime warranty, and real cedar/basswood build quality justifies the premium for buyers committed to long-term wellness use.

For most buyers: **Sanctuary 2 at $8,500-12,000 is the most popular and most defensible pick** — right size for couples and small families, full Clearlight feature set, premium build quality.

For tight budgets: Clearlight Aspire at $4,000-5,000 (entry-tier single-person) or step down to Sun Home Saunas mid-tier.
