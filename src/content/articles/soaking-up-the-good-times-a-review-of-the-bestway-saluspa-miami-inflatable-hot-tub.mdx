## What the Miami actually is

The Bestway SaluSpa Miami AirJet sits at the entry of the SaluSpa lineup:

- **Miami (this model)**: 4-person, ~120 air jets, $350-450
- **Hawaii, Vegas**: similar entry-tier 4-person variants, $300-450
- **Coronado, Helsinki, Palm Springs**: 4-6 person mid-tier, $500-700
- **Bali HydroJet, Cancun HydroJet, Hollywood**: 4-6 person with water jets, $800-1,200

The Miami specifically targets buyers who want to try the inflatable hot tub experience at the lowest reasonable price point that still uses real Bestway pump-and-liner construction. It's not a no-name Amazon clone — it's the same parent brand, same platform, scaled down.

## Setup and install

The Miami's setup is identical to the rest of the SaluSpa line:

1. **Inflate** the tub with the included pump (~10 min)
2. **Connect** the pump unit to the two ports on the tub side
3. **Fill** with garden hose to the marked fill line (~25-30 min for 177 gallons)
4. **Power on** the heater and let it run (18-24 hours from cold to 104°F)

Install requirements:
- **Flat, level surface** — any slope >1° will cause water-level issues
- **Load-bearing surface** — filled Miami weighs ~1,600-1,800 lbs with 2 people; lighter than Coronado but still more than a typical deck rating
- **110V GFCI outlet** within 9 feet (pump cord length)
- **Drainage plan** for the eventual drain (1-2 months for most owners)

The Miami's lower water volume means faster heat-up but also faster temperature loss when the cover is open. If you're using it 1-2 times a week, the smaller water mass works in your favor (faster reheats). For continuous-temperature operation, the smaller volume slightly increases the proportional heat-loss percentage.

## Performance reality

### Jet experience

120 air jets distributed around the perimeter produce the same bubbly champagne sensation as the Coronado. Slightly less aggressive bubble intensity due to lower jet count but the feel is comparable. As with all AirJet models: this is a bubble experience, not a hydromassage experience.

If hydromassage is the goal, the cheapest SaluSpa with water jets is the Bali HydroJet ($700-900) — significantly more cost but materially different jet experience.

### Heat performance

The 2,200W heater is matched to the smaller water volume, so the Miami heats up faster than the Coronado:

- From 60°F to 104°F: ~18-24 hours (vs Coronado's 24-30)
- Maintaining 104°F in 70°F ambient: easy, ~$25-40/month
- Maintaining 104°F in 40°F ambient: ~$60-90/month
- Maintaining 104°F in 20°F ambient: at the heater's limit; temperature may drop overnight

The Miami's smaller water volume gives it a slight advantage in mild climates (faster reheats after use) and a slight disadvantage in cold climates (less thermal mass to retain heat overnight).

### Capacity reality

The "4-person" rating is generous. Real-world:
- **2 adults**: comfortable, jets reach both people, water level stays correct
- **3 adults**: tight but workable, knees nearly touching
- **4 adults**: only if everyone is friendly, water displaces aggressively
- **2 adults + 2 small kids**: perfect, often the realistic use case
- **2 adults + 2 teens**: cramped, the Coronado would be the better choice

Internal usable diameter is ~50 inches when inflated. Two adults at 6-foot height take up most of the available space with comfortable leg extension. The Miami isn't a "family of four" spa in the way some marketing suggests.

## Power bill

The Miami consumes less power than the Coronado due to smaller water volume. Real numbers:

| Climate | Approximate monthly cost (US, $0.15/kWh) |
| --- | --- |
| Mild climate, summer (70-85°F) | $25-40 |
| Temperate, spring/fall (50-70°F) | $40-65 |
| Cold climate, winter (30-50°F) | $65-100 |
| Severe cold (below 30°F sustained) | $100-150 |

At California-grade electricity rates ($0.25-0.40/kWh), multiply by 1.7-2.7x.

Operational tips that apply to all SaluSpas:
- Drop temp to 90-95°F when not in use; ramp back up before use
- Add insulation under the tub (foam pad cuts heat loss to ground by 25-40%)
- Replace OEM cover with aftermarket insulated cover at year 1-2
- Don't run bubble jets unnecessarily — they aerate and cool the water

## Ownership reality

### Year 1: honeymoon

First 12 months are typically issue-free. Most year-1 problems are owner-caused (pets, sharp objects, draining onto plants killed by chlorine).

### Year 2: pump issues

Same as the rest of the SaluSpa lineup — the pump unit is the #1 failure point. Around 18-30 months of regular use:

- **E02 error code** — heater issue, often tripped thermal fuse
- **Reduced flow** — clogged filter or pump motor degradation
- **Loud rattling** — bearing failure

Pump unit is owner-replaceable in ~30 minutes with $120-180 aftermarket replacement. See our [SaluSpa AirJet pump replacement walkthrough](/saluspa-airjet-hot-tub-heater-pump-replacement/) for the step-by-step.

### Year 3-4: replace or repair?

Same math as the Coronado: at year 3 you've spent $350-450 (original) + $150 (pump) + $50-100 (chemicals/filters). A new Miami at year 3 is $350-450 with current-year features. Most owners replace rather than continue repairing.

## Water chemistry — same as the rest

The Miami uses standard chlorine or bromine sanitization, pH control, weekly testing. The smaller water volume (177 gallons vs Coronado's 297) means chemistry shifts faster — plan for daily testing during heavy use periods.

Filter replacement: ~$8 each, every 2-3 weeks. Annual filter cost $60-150.

Full drain interval: every 4-8 weeks depending on use. The smaller volume means buildup happens faster proportionally.

## Bottom line

The SaluSpa Miami is an honest entry-tier inflatable spa — it does exactly what it promises at the price point. The most common buyer mistake is treating the "4-person" capacity rating as literal. For 1-2 person households, the Miami is right-sized and a fair value. For 3+ regular users, the $150-300 upgrade to the Coronado pays for itself in usable space.

If you're entering the inflatable hot tub category for the first time and you're 1-2 people, the Miami is a reasonable starting point — and if you outgrow it in year 2-3, you'll have learned what features matter for your next purchase.
