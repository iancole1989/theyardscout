## What infrared sauna blankets actually are

An infrared sauna blanket is a zippered, sleeping-bag-style enclosure with infrared heating elements embedded in the inner walls. You lie inside it (typically with your head outside the zipper for breathing), it heats to 110-160°F, you sweat for 30-45 minutes, you exit.

Mechanically simple:
- Outer waterproof shell (polyurethane or similar)
- Inner heating layer with infrared panels or carbon-fiber heating elements
- Control unit on a cord (temperature setting, timer)
- Standard 120V household outlet

Compared to cabin saunas:
- **Smaller heating area** — only your body, not the surrounding air
- **Less radiant exposure** — heating elements are on your skin contact, not surrounding panels
- **Lower ambient temp** — blanket interior is hot, but no ambient sauna environment
- **Portable** — folds into a bag, 5-10 lbs typical
- **Plug-and-play** — no install, no dedicated space, no electrician

The session experience is genuinely different from cabin sauna. Cabin = sitting upright in hot air, breathing the heat. Blanket = lying flat in a heated cocoon, head outside the heat. Different feel; some users prefer one or the other.

## Legitimate brands

Three brands worth considering:

### HigherDose ($500-800) — premium tier

HigherDose is the most-marketed sauna blanket brand. NYC-based DTC wellness company, owns the premium positioning in this category.

**Strengths:**
- Premium materials (waterproof shell, silver-charged inner layer)
- Reasonable EMF spec (~3-5 mG at body contact)
- Strong customer service + 1-year warranty
- Travel-friendly bag included
- Brand recognition (visible at gyms, wellness studios)

**Weaknesses:**
- Premium pricing ($500-800)
- Marketing claims about specific health benefits should be taken with appropriate skepticism
- Temperature controls less granular than competitors

**Best for:** Buyers who want the established premium brand, willing to pay $200-300 premium over Sun Home for brand confidence.

### Sun Home Saunas (sauna blanket variant) ($400-600) — mid-tier

Same Sun Home brand as the cabin saunas. Sun Home's blanket competes directly with HigherDose at lower price.

**Strengths:**
- Same Sun Home brand quality + customer service standards
- Cheaper than HigherDose for similar capability
- EMF spec around 2-5 mG
- 5-year warranty (better than HigherDose's 1-year)

**Weaknesses:**
- Less brand recognition in sauna blanket space (more known for cabins)
- Slightly less premium materials than HigherDose

**Best for:** Most blanket buyers — quality is genuinely comparable to HigherDose at $200-300 less.

### SaunaSpace ($800-1,200) — premium with red light

SaunaSpace differentiates with **incandescent bulb heating** rather than carbon fiber — bulbs emit infrared + visible red/near-infrared light. Higher price for the dual function.

**Strengths:**
- Combines infrared sauna effect with red light therapy
- Premium materials
- Strong reputation in biohacking community
- 5-year limited warranty

**Weaknesses:**
- Most expensive in the category
- Bulb-based system is bulkier than carbon-fiber blankets
- Bulb replacement is an ongoing cost (every 1-2 years)

**Best for:** Buyers specifically wanting red light therapy + infrared in one product; biohacking enthusiasts willing to pay premium.

### Avoid — generic Amazon brands ($150-300)

Various unbranded blankets at $150-300 on Amazon. Issues:
- EMF spec often 10-50+ mG (much higher than premium)
- Unknown heating element quality (potential overheating issues)
- 30-90 day return windows only
- No warranty support
- Lifespan typically 6-18 months

The temptation to save $300 vs Sun Home is real. The risk profile (sleeping in a heating element with unknown safety + electrical quality) isn't worth it. Skip the bottom of the market.

## EMF spec reality — why it matters more for blankets

Sauna blankets put heating elements in direct contact with your skin. EMF exposure is more concentrated than cabin saunas where the heating panels are 12-24 inches away from your body.

| Brand tier | Typical EMF at body contact (mG) |
|---|---|
| Premium (HigherDose, Sun Home, SaunaSpace) | 2-5 mG |
| Mid-tier name brands | 5-15 mG |
| Generic Amazon brands | 10-50+ mG |

For occasional users (1-2x/week), the marginal EMF difference between tiers doesn't compound to a meaningful exposure level. For regular users (3-4+ sessions/week long-term), paying for the lower-EMF spec is the operator-honest move.

For very EMF-sensitive users or users with implanted medical devices: consult your physician before using any infrared sauna blanket.

## Session experience — what to actually expect

Standard infrared sauna blanket session:

1. **Pre-heat** the blanket on a stable surface (bed, floor, couch — flat is fine). Pre-heat takes 5-10 minutes.
2. **Enter** — unzip, lie inside on your back, zip up with your head outside the top zipper for breathing.
3. **Wear clothing** that you don't mind sweating in (or use the cotton liner some brands include — protects the blanket from sweat damage).
4. **Hydrate** before and during. Sweating is significant in a working blanket.
5. **Temperature ramp** — heat builds gradually over 15-25 minutes. Most users start sweating around minute 15.
6. **Session length** — 30-45 minutes typical. Some users do 30, some 45+.
7. **Exit + hydrate** — unzip, slowly stand, rehydrate aggressively.

What it feels like vs cabin sauna:
- More enclosed (head-only out)
- Less ambient heat (only your body is heated, not the surrounding air)
- Lower visible sweating during the session, more sweat-soaking after exit
- Sleep + relaxation easier in the lying position (some users specifically do this for sleep prep)

## TCO comparison — blanket vs cabin vs studio

5-year cost of regular infrared use:

| Setup | 5-year total | Per session (at 3/week) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| HigherDose blanket × 5 yr | $700 | $0.90 | Includes ~$200/yr electricity (blankets are efficient) |
| Sun Home blanket × 5 yr | $550 | $0.70 | Same usage profile |
| Sun Home Solstice 2 cabin × 5 yr | $4,500 | $5.80 | Includes ~$300/yr operating cost |
| Studio (3 sessions/wk × $50) | $39,000 | $50 | Each session paid individually |

Blanket wins decisively on TCO. But blanket sessions are not equivalent to cabin sessions or studio sessions. The right framing is:
- Blanket = lying-down sweat session (good for sleep/recovery)
- Cabin = sitting infrared with comprehensive radiation (closer to studio experience)
- Studio = ritual / location-shift / no-maintenance experience

Match the format to the actual use case, not just the cost.

## Bottom line

Infrared sauna blankets are a legitimate format for specific use cases — apartment dwellers, occasional users, travelers, sleep-prep. They're not a direct substitute for cabin saunas, but they're not trying to be.

For most blanket buyers: **Sun Home Saunas blanket at $400-600 is the operator pick** — comparable quality to HigherDose at $200-300 less, with longer warranty.

For premium with red light therapy: SaunaSpace at $800-1,200.

For daily regular use over 5+ year horizons or shared-household use: skip blanket, invest in cabin sauna. The 5-year TCO favors blanket on raw cost, but cabin sessions deliver higher per-session quality and serve multiple users.
