## How we picked these

Six criteria, weighted by what actually matters across a year of ownership:

1. **Build quality / reliability**: pump unit lifespan, liner durability, seam integrity. Established platforms (Bestway, Intex) with widely-available replacement parts won out over generic Amazon brands.
2. **Capacity reality**: not the marketing number ("6-person!"), but how many adults actually fit comfortably. Most marketing overstates by 30-50%.
3. **Jet experience**: AirJet (bubble) vs HydroJet (water) vs combination. Bubble-only is the inflatable norm; water jets are the upgrade tier.
4. **Operating cost**: water capacity affects power consumption. Smaller volume heats faster, larger holds heat better against cold weather.
5. **Replacement parts availability**: SaluSpa/Bestway dominates here. Owner replacing a failed pump at year 2 is the norm, not the exception.
6. **Total cost of ownership** over 3-5 years (purchase + electricity + chemicals + 1 pump replacement): not just sticker price.

## Best inflatable hot tub for each use case

### Best overall — Bestway SaluSpa Coronado ($500-700)

For roughly 70% of buyers, the Coronado is the right pick. It hits the value sweet spot: enough capacity (4-6 person), enough jets (140), enough water volume (297 gallons for proper submersion), and the most mature parts ecosystem in the category. The SaluSpa platform has been refined since 2010 with annual incremental improvements.

What it does well:
- Comfortable for 4 adults regularly (vs Miami's tighter 2-3)
- Heats to 104°F in ~24-30 hours from cold
- ChemConnect dispenser on recent model years simplifies water chemistry
- Insulated cover included
- Replacement pump $120-180 (owner-installable)

Where it falls short:
- AirJet only (no water jets — bubble experience, not hydromassage)
- Pump fails around year 2 (universal SaluSpa pattern)
- Cold-climate operation is fine in mild winters, marginal below 30°F

Deep dive: see our full [SaluSpa Coronado review](/saluspa-coronado/).

### Best for 1-2 person households — Bestway SaluSpa Miami ($350-450)

If you're a couple or single user with budget under $500, the Miami is the operator pick. Same Bestway pump platform as the Coronado, smaller capacity. The size feels noticeably tighter inside, but for 1-2 person use it's exactly right.

Better than Coronado for the 2-person use case:
- 30-50% less water heating cost (smaller volume)
- 18-24 hour heat-up vs Coronado's 24-30
- Smaller footprint for tight outdoor spaces
- Lower failure exposure (smaller liner = fewer failure points)

Don't pick if:
- 3+ regular users (you'll regret the size choice within 1 season)
- Frequent entertaining (Coronado is the entertaining pick)
- Cold climate (less thermal mass means harder time holding temp in winter)

Deep dive: [SaluSpa Miami review](/soaking-up-the-good-times-a-review-of-the-bestway-saluspa-miami-inflatable-hot-tub/).

### Best with water jets — Bestway SaluSpa Bali HydroJet ($700-900)

The Bali HydroJet bridges the gap between inflatable and hard-sided. Adds 4-6 dedicated water jets to the standard AirJet array. The water jets actually deliver targeted pressure massage — meaningfully different from the bubble-only experience.

Worth the premium over Coronado for:
- Real hydromassage on neck, back, lumbar
- Hybrid jet experience (bubbles + targeted water)
- Same 4-6 person capacity
- Same Bestway platform reliability

Not worth the premium if:
- You'll be happy with bubble jets only (saves $200+ to go Coronado)
- You're really shopping for hydromassage and have $4,000+ budget for entry hard-sided (better hydromassage on hard-sided spas)

### Best premium aesthetics — MSpa Camaro ($900-1,200)

MSpa differentiates on visual design — square shapes vs Bestway's rounded, premium-looking exteriors, modern aesthetic. Inside the cabin, the experience is comparable to similar-tier SaluSpas. You're paying for the look, not for functional differences.

Worth picking if:
- Outdoor aesthetic matters more than 20-30% lower price
- You want something that doesn't look like a "pool toy"
- You'll display it prominently rather than tuck it away

Skip if budget matters — for $300-500 less you get equivalent function on Bestway.

## Operating cost reality across the picks

Inflatable hot tubs are NOT cheap to run despite being cheap to buy. The "pool toy" reputation hides real operating costs.

| Climate | Coronado-class monthly | Miami-class monthly | Bali HydroJet monthly |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mild summer (70°F+) | $40-65 | $35-55 | $45-70 |
| Temperate (50-70°F) | $60-90 | $50-80 | $65-95 |
| Cold winter (30-50°F) | $90-150 | $80-130 | $100-160 |
| Severe cold (under 30°F) | $130-200 | $110-180 | $140-220 |

Annual cost (temperate climate): $700-1,200. Cold climate: $1,000-2,000. Operating cost matters as much as purchase price for total ownership economics.

## What to budget for as an owner

Beyond purchase, year-1 costs:
- **Foam insulation pad** ($30-100): cuts heat loss to ground by 25-40%. Single biggest operating-cost reducer.
- **Aftermarket insulated cover** ($80-150 by year 1-2): OEM cover insulation degrades. Aluminum-backed aftermarket cover is the upgrade.
- **Pump replacement** ($120-180 around year 2): SaluSpa lifecycle item, owner-replaceable.
- **Chemistry + filters** ($150-250/year): chlorine/bromine sanitizer, pH adjusters, filter cartridges every 2-3 weeks.

3-year total cost for a Coronado at typical use:
- Purchase: $600
- Foam pad + better cover: $150
- Operating (3 years × $1,000/year): $3,000
- Pump replacement: $150
- Chemistry + filters (3 years × $200/year): $600
- **Total 3-year cost: ~$4,500**

For comparison, entry hard-sided spa over 3 years: ~$7,500. The 3-year inflatable math favors inflatable. Over 10 years the math flips (multiple pump replacements + liner replacements + accumulated operating costs).

## Bottom line

The 2026 inflatable hot tub landscape has three credible brands (Bestway, Intex, MSpa) and roughly 12 active models. Most buyers should pick from the Bestway SaluSpa lineup:

- **Coronado** at $500-700 for most use cases
- **Miami** at $350-450 for 1-2 person households
- **Bali HydroJet** at $700-900 for water-jet preference

Skip inflatable entirely if you're committing to daily long-term wellness use, have a sustained-cold climate, or want hard-sided hydromassage quality. At those use cases, the $4,000-8,000 entry hard-sided tier wins on 5-7 year TCO math.
